When I started placement preparation, I thought collecting many resources meant progress. Later I realized I was not improving because I had effort but no clear direction.

<!-- truncate -->

Everything changed once I simplified my workflow and focused on consistency.

## What was not working

I was:
- Switching topics too often
- Spending more time planning than solving
- Jumping across too many platforms

This created confusion and reduced confidence.

## What changed my progress

I made three practical decisions:
1. Kept platforms limited to LeetCode and GeeksforGeeks
2. Followed a fixed topic order instead of random problem solving
3. Measured consistency, not just daily volume

## My DSA learning order

I followed this sequence to build problem-solving confidence:
1. Arrays and strings
2. Hashing and sliding window
3. Linked lists
4. Trees and recursion

For each problem, I used this method:
- Think independently for a fixed time
- Study the solution if blocked
- Rewrite the complete logic in my own way

Rewriting was the step that made concepts stick.

## Balancing DSA, development, and core CS

I learned that placement preparation is not only DSA.

Along with problem solving, I worked on:
- Web development projects (frontend and backend thinking)
- Core CS concepts (OS, DBMS, and basic networking)

This helped me handle interview conversations beyond coding questions.

## Interview mindset that helped me

By the time I attended the Infosys process, I was not overprepared or underprepared. I was stable in fundamentals.

What helped most:
- Staying calm
- Reading questions carefully
- Recognizing common patterns (sliding window, two pointers, traversals)

## Final advice

Keep the process simple:
- Pick a clear path
- Practice consistently
- Improve gradually

Small daily progress compounds. It may not be visible in the first few weeks, but it shows up strongly during real interviews.

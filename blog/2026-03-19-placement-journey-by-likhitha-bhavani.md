Placements are one of the most important phases in a student's life. I still remember when I had no idea where to start, what to study, or how to prepare.

<!-- truncate -->

With consistency and a clear approach, I was able to crack the placement process and secure a job at Infosys. This is my journey, the challenges I faced, and what helped me succeed.

## How it all started

I did not start placement preparation early. In my third year, my primary goal was to crack GATE and get into a top IIT. I prepared core subjects such as Operating Systems, DBMS, Computer Networks, and aptitude.

Later, I realized the importance of placements and shifted focus to job preparation. At that time, I did not have strong DSA skills or clarity on domains like machine learning and full stack, so I started seriously from basics.

## What I focused on

Most companies tested these areas:
- Aptitude and reasoning
- DSA (Data Structures and Algorithms)
- SQL
- Core CS subjects
- Resume-based questions

## Aptitude preparation

I started with aptitude and reasoning because they are common in most company processes.

Key topics:
- Quantitative: percentages, profit and loss, time and work, ratio
- Reasoning: puzzles, seating arrangement, coding-decoding
- Verbal: reading comprehension, synonyms

Resources:
- Websites: IndiaBIX, PrepInsta, GeeksforGeeks
- YouTube: CareerRide, AKAgarwal Aptitude, Feel Free to Learn, Current Affairs Funda

At first, it was difficult, but daily practice improved my speed and accuracy.

## DSA preparation

Initially, I struggled because I tried solving problems without strong fundamentals. Later, I focused on core topics first: arrays, strings, linked lists, stacks, queues, and time/space complexity.

Resources:
- Striver's A2Z DSA Sheet for a structured roadmap
- Code and Debug (YouTube) for concept clarity
- Practice platforms: LeetCode and HackerRank, starting with easy and then moving to medium problems

Important topics I practiced:
- Arrays, recursion, sorting
- Trees, graphs
- Dynamic programming
- Greedy algorithms

Consistent practice improved both my problem-solving skill and confidence.

## SQL learning

I started with basic tutorials and then moved to regular practice.

Resources:
- YouTube: freeCodeCamp, Apna College, CodeWithHarry
- Practice: SQLBolt, HackerRank, LeetCode

Key topics:
- `SELECT`, `WHERE`
- `JOIN`s (very important)
- `GROUP BY`, `HAVING`
- Subqueries

## Core CS subjects

Core subjects played an important role, especially in MNC interviews.

Subjects covered:
- DBMS
- Operating Systems
- Computer Networks
- OOP concepts

Resources:
- Viswadeep Gothi (Operating Systems)
- Amit Khurana (DBMS and Computer Networks)

Strong fundamentals helped me perform better in interviews.

## Resume building

A strong resume is very important. I followed these rules:
- Keep it simple and clear
- Highlight key skills and projects
- Make it ATS-friendly
- Use Overleaf for clean formatting
- Be confident about everything included

Interviewers can ask deep questions from your resume, so project clarity matters a lot.

## Challenges I faced

- Lack of confidence in the beginning
- Difficulty in solving coding problems
- Managing time with academics

Consistency helped me overcome these challenges.

## Final learning

This journey taught me:
- Consistency is more important than talent
- Practice and patience always pay off

## Tips for juniors

- Start early, but if you have not started yet, start now
- Focus on basics
- Practice daily
- Do not compare your journey with others
- Stay confident and positive

This was my journey from confusion to confidence. If I can do it, you can too.
